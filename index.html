Praire Protection Colorado

A redesign of the Prairie Protection Colorado's (PPC) website to prevent the praire dogs from fending for themselves

Prairie Protection Colorado(PPC) is a non-profit organization that advocates for prairie dogs by drawing attention to the mass exterminations of prairie dog colonies through organizing and resisting the destruction of the last remaining prairie communities. Their website was designed to promote awareness around prairie dog endangerment and allow users to support their cause online.

Overview

Problem

Solution

My team and I observed that users often have trouble finding the information they need and found the unprofessional/sketchy look of the PPC’s website off-putting, preventing users from engaging with PPC in meaningful ways like volunteering or working with PPC to save their own local colony. This results in missed opportunities for involvement from potential supporters—leaving the prairie dogs to fend for themselves.

The solution is to redesign and organize the PPC’s website so that (1) our users feel empowered and informed about the PPC and its cause, (2) users can easily find the information they need, and (3) get users returning to the website.

Research

User Interviews

Our initial approach to conducting our user interviews was to gather insight on what components/factors would make a nonprofit organization seem legitimate/reliable based on their website. I identified to my team while that was useful information, we could also add on a usability test to discover key weaknesses about the website that our team could improve.

We conducted qualitative interviews + usability tests with 5 participants and found:

What users were looking for:

  • Sleek, modern, and updated design
  • No Ads
  • Thoughtful and organized navigation
  • Donation call to action button

User insights about the website:

  • Lack of readability
  • Navigation bar affordances
  • Non professional presentation

Stakeholder interview

During our stakeholder interview, with Deanna Meyer Treasurer of PPC, our goal was to find out what PPC need their website to do for them, and they wanted the following:

  1. To find more actionable activists the join the fight not only for the prairie dog, but all of the wildlife along the Rocky Mountains that are being encroached upon due to developments.
  2. To obtain more donations to use in processes of saving the prairie dogs.
  3. To have an overall more professional, sleek, and inviting look to the website

After interviewing our users and stakeholder, we established our user persona, River, to embody our users and their needs.

Competitor Analysis

I conducted a direct competitor analysis with Prairie Dog Coalition and Prairie Dog Pals. We also analyzed some indirect competitors: GoFundMe and Denver Urban Garden.

Takeaways from our Competitors:

  • Hero image
    • We can utilize a hero image to encase PPC’s mission statement. This would allow users to immediately see their mission statement and have a central idea of not only what the organization is about but also an idea of what they might expect when visiting the website.
  • Content chunking
    • Because the content in PPC’s website is not sectioned out, it makes it difficult for users to digest the information their reading which can be off-putting. This further prevents users from being informed about prairie dogs and how to protect them, leaving them to fend for themselves. Content chunking on the PPC’s website would allow users to easily digest the information and be fully informed about what they’re reading.
  • “Call to Action” buttons
    • Some buttons on PPC’s website are not active and can cause confusion to users what is and isn’t clickable. By adding “call to action” button, this gives the user a straightforward approach on what they can do.

Feature Priority Matrix

Based on the feedback we got from users and stakeholder, we prioritized these main features:

  1. A more sensible navigation bar to help users find what they need
  2. UI: Typography hierarchy, fonts, page content into separate blocks
  3. Easier way to find social media/newsletter sign-ups

User Flow

Because this was a multifaceted website, we wanted to focus back on our user persona, River, and her main goal: To save a prairie colony near her home. We revolved our re-design around this, so that River could intuitively navigate the website and easily save a colony in just a few clicks.
